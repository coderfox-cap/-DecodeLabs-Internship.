This project analyzes 2.5 years of e-commerce transactional data to uncover the factors driving revenue performance, customer purchasing behavior, marketing-channel effectiveness, and operational inefficiencies. Using Python, Pandas, DuckDB, SQL, Seaborn, and Matplotlib, the analysis combines statistical exploration, visualization, and business reasoning to transform raw transactional records into actionable insights.
The study reveals that revenue fluctuations are primarily driven by changes in customer spending behavior rather than variations in demand volume. Marketing-channel analysis highlights significant differences in customer quality across acquisition sources, while operational analysis identifies elevated cancellation tendencies among specific channels. Revenue-concentration analysis further demonstrates a relatively diversified revenue structure, reducing dependence on a small number of high-value transactions
